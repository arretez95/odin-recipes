Hack Noodle Soup

Description

Is it a cold day today? Or rainy? Or perhaps you don't know what to cook and just want to throw everything into a pot and be done with it. I give you... Arretez-style noodle soup! This can be modified in any way, shape, or form, because I literally just threw whatever we had into a pot. No exact measurements needed. If you like an ingredient, put more of it. And vice versa if there's something you dislike. That's it!

Ingredients

  • Frozen Sanuki udon, thawed (or your preferred noodle, cooked to package direction)
  • Thin-sliced meat of your preference
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/3 tomato
  • 1 green onion stalk
  • Water or broth of your preference
  • Bouillon
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 rosemary sprig
  • soy sauce

Steps

  1. In a clay pot, add onion, green onion, and tomato into pot. Fill with water (or broth, if using) to cover. Heat until boiling, then bring down to a simmer. Wait ten minutes, then cook to taste.
  2. While waiting for soup to boil, in a separate bowl, mix soy sauce into the thawed udon noodles. If using dry noodle or pasta, cook partially, drain, then mix with soy sauce.
  3. Add noodles, thin-sliced meat, butter, bouillon, and rosemary to soup and bring back to a boil. Cook till noodles are al dente or preferred consistency. Salt and pepper to taste.
  4. (optional) Remove tomatoes, onions, green onions, and rosemary from soup. These can be topped with salt and pepper and served on the side.
  5. Enjoy directly from the pot!
